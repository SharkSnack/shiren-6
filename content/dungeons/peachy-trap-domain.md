Dungeon where you start with a Peach Club and Trapper's Bracelet, and use Peach Buns and traps to progress.<br/>Trapper status lets the player pick up traps and set them on the ground to ensnare monsters,<br/>but traps can't be picked up again once set, and Trapper status is lost while Trapper's Bracelet is unequipped.<br/>Peach Club is a weapon that has a chance to transform a defeated monster into a Peach Bun,<br/>and eating a Peach Bun morphs the player into the corresponding monster, allowing usage of its abilities.<br/>Overall, this dungeon is like an intersection of [Trapper's Secret Path](/dungeons/trapper's-secret-path) and [Peach Dungeon](/dungeons/peach-dungeon) with a unique flavor,<br/>slightly reminiscent of past dungeons like Kitchen God Shrine (Shiren 1) and Gen's Turf (Shiren 5).

# Strategy

### General

Peachy Trap Domain fully expects the player to utilize both Peach Buns and Traps to progress.<br/>It's possible to discard the starting gear, but you're likely to starve or be overwhelmed by strong foes,<br/>since the monster table features out-of-depth enemies like Hat Brat (3-10F) and N'dama (15-19F) quite early.<br/>That said, the aggressive monster table is manageable as long as you make use of Peach Buns and traps.<br/>Certain floors feature multiple levels of the same monster family on a single floor, so being able to tell<br/>the higher level monsters apart from their lower level counterparts is important to avoid accidents.

Pitfall Traps, Floor-exiting Traps, and Bottomless Pot don't appear in this dungeon.<br/>Food and incenses are never generated as ground items, and Trapper's Bracelet is likely the only bracelet.<br/>Staves are generally shop-exclusive except for Peach Staff which can be obtained from enemies.

The most consistent strategy involves eating a Hat Urchin Bun to collect Peach Staves on 1-2F,<br/>synthesizing staves and equipment during the first Mixer (16-30F) zone, and then focusing on obtaining<br/>useful Peach Buns and traps to rush to the stairs for the remainder of the dungeon.

#### Normal Status Pros/Cons

- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Insert or take out items from pots.
- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Equip Trapper's Bracelet to reveal hidden traps, pick them up and set them down.
- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Equip Peach Club and defeat monsters for a roughly 30% chance to obtain a Peach Bun.
- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) If synthesis goes well, you can combine attacking with trapping to power through encounters.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Tends to have lower defense than Break-Off set + Morphed status.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Peach Staff is often your only method to deal with distant threats.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Invincible Grass or Swift Grass are often your single room Monster House survival options.

#### Morphed Status Pros/Cons

- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Equip the Break-Off set before transforming for 30 attack/defense that won't degrade.
- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Gain access to a powerful special attack, depending on the Peach Bun you eat.
- (<span class="greenText">+</span>) Lots of options for survivng a single room Monster House.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Need to end Morphed status to organize your inventory after farming.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Equipment runes are nullified, so you need to end Morphed status to obtain Peach Buns.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) You're susceptible to hidden traps, and you can't ensnare monsters in traps.
- (<span class="redText">-</span>) Can be hard to stock Peach Buns if your luck with Preservation Pots is bad.

### Status Explanations

#### Morphed Status

- Shiren's stats (HP, attack, defense) remain the same during Morphed status.
    - In other words, eating a Mamel Bun won't set your stats to be the same as a Mamel.
- Equipment runes and bracelet effects are nullified during Morphed status.
    - Increased attack and defense from equipment remains in effect.
    - Trapper status won't be active - don't forget where traps were before transforming.
- Reverting from Morphed status doesn't end the player's turn.
- All status conditions are cured when Shiren eats a Peach Bun or ends Morphed status.
    - This includes both positive and negative status conditions.

#### Trapper Status

- Shiren's color palette changes to purple to indicate that Trapper status is active.
- All hidden traps are revealed, and traps can be picked up, set on the ground, and thrown.
- Monsters can be ensnared by traps, and traps don't activate when Shiren walks on them.
    - However, select monsters like the Floor Dragon family and Floating types can't be trapped.
- Shiren earns 10% of the regular amount of experience points when a monster steps on a trap.
- Shiren earns x2.0 experience points when a monster is slain by a trap that deals damage.
    - Traps that one-shot monsters like Rotting Trap do not grant experience points.

### Advanced Strategies

#### Peach Bun Duplication

##### Method 1 - Bouncy Wall Room

Throw the Peach Bun in a way where it bounces off of all 4 sides of the room to duplicate it.<br/>However, some Bouncy Wall layouts that get generated are impossible to complete.

##### Method 2 - Far-throwing Trap + Peach Staff

1. Step on a Far-throwing Trap to gain Far-throwing status.
2. Line up a bunch of monsters either in a hallway or large room.
    - Single room Monster Houses or 2-room map layouts work the best.
3. Throw a Peach Bun at the line of enemies to turn them all into the target monster.
4. Throw a Peach Staff[0] at the line of monsters to turn them all into Peach Buns.

#### Peach Bun Level Up

1. Throw a Peach Bun at an enemy to turn them into the target monster.
2. Level the monster up using a Blinding Trap, Ghost Samurai, or a cannon's friendly fire.
3. Swing a Peach Staff to turn the target monster back into a Peach Bun.

### Important Items

#### Weapon / Shield / Bracelets

Have a Removal Trap or Fixer Scroll on hand before equipping other items in case the item is cursed.

<p><b><u>Peach Club / Trapper's Bracelet</u></b><br/>Starting equipment, and be very careful not to lose either item.<br/>Beware of Swordmaster (60-70F) in particular if you're attempting the 99F version.</p>

<p><b><u>Break-Off Blade / Break-Off Shield</u></b><br/>Best equipment for Morphed status, since the Dulling rune gets nullified during transformation.</p>

<p><b><u>Manji Kabura / Fuuma Shield (or Doutanuki / Wolfshead)</u></b><br/>Any set with resonance offers higher attack and defense than just a Peach Club,<br/>but Manji Kabura and Fuuma Shield are the most powerful among resonance options.<br/>Doutanuki and Wolfshead is a drop in power, but offers an extra rune slot over the strongest set.</p>

<p><b><u>Axe of the Minotaur</u></b><br/>The Critical rune is a significant boost to your offense when you don't have Morphed status.<br/>Obtained from Minotaur (16-18F), Megataur (31-40F), or Gigataur (60-99F).</p>

<p><b><u>Spearscale Shield</u></b><br/>Can be nice to protect Peach Buns from Water Ninja's special attack.<br/>Obtained from Spearfish (1-2F), Lt. Spearfish (13-14F), Capt. Spearfish (24-25F), or Admiral Spearfish (53-68F).</p>

#### Projectiles

<p><b><u>Iron Arrow</u></b><br/>Quite tricky to obtain in this dungeon, but can be decent if strength is high.<br/>Level down a Mini Tank using a Skullmancer's magic bullet on 41F, then swing a Peach Staff.</p>

<p><b><u>Porky's Rock</u></b><br/>Throw Porky's Rocks to one-shot Gitan Mamels (55F, 65F, 75F, 85F) and Ghost Samurais.<br/>Obtained by eating a Porgon Bun between 66-70F.</p>

#### Grass / Food

<p><b><u>Revival Grass</u></b><br/>The floor table is limited for grasses, so Lv2-4 Hat Urchin family Peach Buns can mass produce this grass.<br/>Don't carry an excessive number of Revival Grass, since you also need space for Peach Buns, traps, etc.</p>

<p><b><u>Invincible Grass / Swift Grass</u></b><br/>Obtained by using Lv2-4 Hat Urchin family Peach Buns.<br/>These serve as a counter to single room Monster Houses, so be sure to keep some on hand.<br/>Other options for surviving such Monster Houses often comes down to Peach Buns of Swift monsters.</p>

<p><b><u>Strength Grass</u></b><br/>Obtained by using Lv2-4 Hat Urchin family Peach Buns.<br/>If you farm a ton of this grass, you might be able to trade hits with stronger monsters.</p>

<p><b><u>Grilled Onigiri</u></b><br/>Obtained directly by chaining Onigiri Trap (or Onigiri Payback) + Blasting Trap or Explosion Trap,<br/>or indirectly by utilizing Rotting Trap + Blasting Trap / Dragon / Fire Ninja / Pop Tank / Flamepuff.<br/>You'll need to eat 23 Grilled Onigiri (+2 Max Fullness) if you want to activate Sumo status,<br/>assuming you immediately eat the Large Onigiri you start with for +4 Max Fullness.</p>

#### Scrolls

<p><b><u>Mapping Scroll</u></b><br/>Read this at the start of a floor when you plan to farm items or duplicate Peach Buns.<br/>If you find these later on in the 99F version, read them between 51-74F or on Gitan Mamel floors.</p>

<p><b><u>Sanctuary Scroll</u></b><br/>Safely farm items using Morphed status while standing on the placed Sanctuary Scroll.<br/>That said, it can also be used to counter single room Monster Houses, so consider saving it early on.<br/>Hat Rascal Bun can steal more Sanctuary Scrolls, but you need to be in a shop to obtain usable ones,<br/>since otherwise the currently placed Sanctuary Scroll vanishes when a new one is tossed to the ground.</p>

<p><b><u>Water-drain Scroll / Wall-less Scroll</u></b><br/>Can be used to recover items that fall in water due to Armordillo's tackle.</p>

<p><b><u>Trap-eraser Scroll</u></b><br/>Read before farming items using Morphed status, but detrimental if you have Trapper status.</p>

<p><b><u>Fixer Scroll</u></b><br/>Fully restore HP, remove curses from equipment, paralyze enemies when surrounded, etc.<br/>Paralyzing effect can be used to aid leveling up a target monster via Ghost Samurai.</p>

<p><b><u>Trap Scroll</u></b><br/>Shop-exclusive. Generate more traps for use with Trapper status.</p>

#### Staves

<p><b><u>Peach Staff</u></b><br/>Obtained by using Lv1-4 Hat Urchin family Peach Buns.<br/>This is the only staff that can be stolen using Hat Urchin's ability during Morphed status.<br/>Synthesize a number of Peach Staves to save space, but always check for curses beforehand.<br/>Some players like to identify the synthesized Peach Staff, even at the cost of using a Blank Scroll.<br/>Keep some low use count staves on hand too, since they can be used to duplicate Peach Buns.</p>

<p><b><u>Other Staves</u></b><br/>Shop-exclusive - Narrow-escape, Paralyzing, Switching, Earthmound, Disguising, etc.<br/>Status inflicting staves are useful, but beware of Okame Monk curing status conditions.</p>

#### Pots / Incenses

<p><b><u>Preservation Pot</u></b><br/>Top priority pot that lets you carry more Peach Buns, traps, staves, and other items.<br/>Pot-upsizing Scroll can't be found, but you might be able to duplicate it via Bouncy Wall Rooms.</p>

<p><b><u>Exorcism Pot / Identifier Pot / Ordinary Pot</u></b><br/>Insert Revival Grass or items that you otherwise won't need to use right away.<br/>Exorcism Scroll is shop-exclusive, so be a bit careful with how you handle Exorcism Pots.</p>

# Floor Guide

<h3 class="heading3First">First Clear (1-50F)</h3>

### 1-2F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> Hat Urchin (1-2F), Death Reaper (1-2F), Shagga (1-2F), Pit Mamel (2-3F)</p>

<p><span class="purpleText2">Peach Staves</span> & <span class="blueText">Leveling</span> Zone - Hat Urchin Bun for staves, Pit Mamel + Blinding Trap for Cave Mamel.<br/>Peach Staff is the only item you can steal while morphed as Hat Urchin, and the theft always succeeds.<br/>Equip a shield before transforming if possible - Break-Off Shield won't degrade during Morphed status.<br/>It might seem wasteful, but it's best to swing each staff you obtain 1 time to check for curses.<br/>Stock extra Hat Urchin Buns if you want to collect more Peach Staves later on.</p>

If you want to be extra safe, reveal hidden traps in every room by attacking above traps before morphing.<br/>This technique ensures that all traps remain visible during Morphed status (or read a Trap-eraser Scroll).

If you find a Blinding Trap, lure a Pit Mamel onto it and create a Cave Mamel or Gitan Mamel,<br/>then hunt it using a Rockfall Trap or Boulder Trap to earn lots of experience points.<br/>If you lack a Blinding Trap, throw Rotten Onigiri at Pit Mamels to potentially Blind them instead.<br/>And if that also isn't an option, stock up on Pit Mamel Buns and level them up on a different floor.

Otherwise, Death Reaper Bun grants Swift speed, which is great for Monster Houses.<br/>They can also be leveled up into Soul Reaper Buns a little bit later for wallpass if desired.<br/>Shagga Bun is another strong option if leveled up, since higher levels grant additional attacks.

If you spot a Far-throwing Trap, go ahead and collect it to perform Peach Bun duplication later on.<br/>Remember that you'll need some Peach Staves to throw under Far-throwing status for this trick,<br/>with Peach Staff[0] being the ideal one to throw since it doesn't waste any staff charges.

### 3-9F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> Pit Mamel (2-3F), Armordillo (3-4F), Pumphantasm (3-4F), Froggo (4F), <span class="redText">Hat Brat</span> (3-10F),<br/>Fire Ninja D (5-8F), Water Ninja D (7-9F)</p>

<p><span class="purpleText2">Farming</span> & <span class="blueText">Leveling</span> Zone - Another floor range where you can linger until the wind blows.<br/>Hat Brat (125 HP, 45 atk, 9 def) is rare, but it's very strong for how early it appears in this dungeon.<br/>You'll definitely want to fight it with traps or Peach Bun-related mechanics to survive any encounters,<br/>with the ideal option being swinging a Peach Staff and using its Peach Bun to collect grasses.<br/>The best obtainable grasses include Revival Grass, Strength Grass, Invincible Grass, and Swift Grass,<br/>so it's worth the effort to linger and farm until the wind blows despite the risk.</p>

Armordillo Bun can be used to generate items, though it's chance-based instead of being guaranteed.<br/>Pumphantasm Bun can be used to pass through walls, and Froggo Bun can be used to stockpile Gitan.<br/>Fire Ninja D Bun can be used to grill Rotten Onigiri, and Water Ninja D Bun can be used to traverse water tiles.<br/>※ An enemy Water Ninja D's special attack can make Peach Buns outside of pots rot.

Explosion Trap → Boulder Trap can be used to earn 1,760 experience points from a Hat Brat,<br/>but it's only recommended if you missed out on creating a Cave Mamel or Gitan Mamel earlier,<br/>since it's usually better to turn Hat Brats into Peach Buns using a Peach Staff in order to farm grass.

### 10-14F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Hat Brat</span> (3-10F), Masked Samurai (10-12F), Pot Knave (10-12F), Mudkin (11-14F)</p>

<p><span class="purpleText2">Peach Bun Level Up</span> Zone - Ghost Samurai can be used to level up Peach Buns here.<br/>For steps on how to level up a Peach Bun, check the <a href="#advanced-strategies">Advanced Strategies</a> section of this page.<br/>Keep in mind that you can stock up on Ghost Samurai Buns to level up other Peach Buns later on.<br/>Expect inventory space to be quite limited at this point if you stole lots of Peach Staves.</p>

Throw Pit Mamel Buns at foes and level them into Cave Mamels via Ghost Samurai to reach Lv20+.<br/>Mudkin can also be leveled up to Lv3-4 and then punched for experience points if you're bored,<br/>but it might be faster to use traps against higher level monsters via Ghost Samurai instead:<br/>Pesky Kappa (330), Famed Octopling (420), Mudster (460), Mudder (920), Hat Rascal (1080)<br/>※ The above Exp values get doubled when you slay the target with a damage dealing trap.

Throw an Eradication Scroll at Pot Knave (65 HP, 35 atk, 25 def) if you have a Blank Scroll,<br/>since otherwise Bag Knave (23-30F) and Nimble Knave (51-61F) can be a menace later on.<br/>Other monsters include Tiger Thrower, Metalhead, Floor Dragon, and Lt. Spearfish.<br/>If you want the Waterproof rune, linger and hunt Lt. Spearfish for a Spearscale Shield drop.

### 15-19F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">N'dama</span> (15-19F), <span class="redText">Lv2 Monks</span> (15-19F), Lv1 Monks (16-19F), Hat Prankster (16-17F),<br/>Egg Thing (16-17F), Thiefwalrus (16-19F), Mixer (16-30F), <span class="redText">Mixergon</span> (16-30F), N'dubba (18-21F)</p>

<p><span class="blueText">Synthesis</span> & <span class="purpleText2">Farming</span> Zone - Stronger monsters begin to appear, but it's still a great time to linger.<br/>Hat Prankster Buns and Thiefwalrus Buns can both be used to generate additional items if desired.<br/>Egg Thing Bun can be leveled up into a Flying or Hiding Egg Bun, then duplicated for Huge or Special Onigiri.<br/>Pop Tank (16-18F)'s cannonballs and Flamepuff (18-20F)'s special attack can grill onigiri.</p>

Lv1-2 monks also appear and Lv2 monks hit pretty hard, so look closely at the color of their shell.<br/>A white-colored shell is a Lv1 (D rank) monk, and a brown-colored shell is a Lv2 (C rank) monk.<br/>Different levels of the same monster family appearing at the same time is a theme for this dungeon,<br/>so it's important to pay attention to the monster's colors to tell higher level ones apart.

Mixer (78 HP, 39 atk, 29 def) and Mixergon (118 HP, 68 atk, 40 def) provide a chance to synthesize items.<br/>Mixers and Mixergons aren't that uncommon here, so feel free to combine staves to free up inventory space.<br/>A single Mixer can be used twice if you turn it into a Peach Bun using a Peach Club after throwing items,<br/>though this trick doesn't work if you swing a Peach Staff instead.

N'dama (135 HP, 50 atk, 35 def) won't be revealed even when inserting into a pot or advancing floors,<br/>so only use newly found items when your HP is topped up, and be ready to swing a Peach Staff.<br/>If you want a N'dubba family Peach Bun for Hiding status, hunt N'dubba on 18-21F instead.

#### N'dama Tips

Sample guidelines for handling items on N'dama floors:

- Staves - Swing the staff 1 time when your surroundings are safe.
- Grass - Eat or throw the grass at a monster instead of carrying it in your inventory.
- Pot - It's probably best to ignore pots that aren't Preservation Pots or "Tap" type pots.
- Blank Scroll - Write Heavenly, Earthly, or Pot-upsize and try using it to upgrade right away.
- If you want to carry the item, insert it into a dedicated Preservation Pot named "N'dama?".

### 20-29F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> Mixer (16-30F), <span class="redText">Mixergon</span> (16-30F), <span class="redText">Lashagga</span> (20-24F), <span class="redText">Curse Matron</span> (21-30F), <span class="redText">Great Samurai</span> (22-30F),<br/><span class="redText">Bag Knave</span> (23-30F), Dark Owl (24-25F), Daze Radish (26-29F), Greenwalrus (26-29F), Dragon (27-32F)</p>

<p><span class="greenText">Mid Game Begins</span> - This is where you want to wrap up a majority of your farming and synthesis.<br/>The monster table is a combo of Firespew Mountain ~ Mount Jatou enemies + some out-of-depth foes.<br/>Fire Ninja C (20-23F), Bag Knave (23-30F), and Pesky Kappa (24-27F) can destroy items on the ground.<br/>Water Ninja C (22-23F) can rot Peach Buns outside of pots, and Super Gazer (22-24F) can cause item loss.<br/>Lashagga and Great Samurai's attack power can be reduced to mostly harmless levels by using a Rust Trap.<br/>Vile Scorpion (108 HP, 45 atk, 33 def) appears on 29-32F, but you won't lose strength during Morphed status.<br/>25F is always a Shuffle Dungeon style map that looks somewhat like Suzunari Pass.</p>

This floor range is riskier, but Great Samurai can be used to level up Peach Buns if desired.<br/>Confusion Grass can be obtained from Daze Radish → synthesized with a weapon for the Confusing rune.<br/>If you use a Ghost Samurai to level up a Daze Radish into a Sleep Radish and then swing a Peach Staff,<br/>Sedating Grass can be obtained as well → synthesized with a weapon for the Sedating rune.<br/>Greenwalrus Bun lets you collect items, Dark Owl Bun grants Mapping Scroll effect + Tiptoe status.<br/>Great Samurai Bun serves as an extra life, provided you remember to eat it before collapsing.<br/>Dragon (120 HP, 45 atk, 40 def)'s fire can be utilized to grill Rotten Onigiri.

Lashagga (99 HP, 43 atk, 34 def) performs 3 consecutive normal attacks in a single turn.<br/>Great Samurai (149 HP, 58 atk, 40 def) revives as Ghost Samurai (4 HP) after being defeated.<br/>Curse Matron (145 HP, 53 atk, 48 def) curses up to 2 of equipped weapon, shield, or bracelets at once.<br/>Bag Knave (95 HP, 40 atk, 35 def) throws Weeds which parries an inventory item, knocking it away.

### 30-32F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> Mixer (16-30F), <span class="redText">Mixergon</span> (16-30F), <span class="redText">Curse Matron</span> (21-30F), <span class="redText">Great Samurai</span> (22-30F),<br/><span class="redText">Bag Knave</span> (23-30F), Dragon (27-32F), Vile Scorpion (29-32F), <span class="redText">Mini Tank</span> (30-31F), Pumplich (30-32F),<br/>Polygon Shaka (30-35F), <span class="redText">Polygon Singa</span> (31-50F), <span class="redText">Megataur</span> (31-40F)</p>

<p><span class="blueText">Last Chance for Synthesis (1st clear)</span> - The next time Mixer family monsters appear after 30F is 60-79F.<br/>Polygon Shaka (yellow, lowers Fullness) overlaps with Polygon Singa (blue, lowers Max Fullness) on 31-35F.<br/>Megataur (170 HP, 55 atk, 42 def) can only attack when it's adjacent, but it's quite formidable.<br/>That said, try to save items for Polygon Singas and upcoming Skullmancers instead when possible.<br/>Overall, it's best to start rushing to the stairs from this point if aiming for the 50F clear.</p>

Mini Tank (100 HP, 55 atk, 40 def) has Swift 1 speed, and shoots Silver Arrows when lined up in a room.<br/>Mini Tank transforms into Pop Tank if it levels up, so keep that in mind if you get caught in a room.<br/>Otherwise, lure it into a hallway so that it doesn't attack, and stock up on Mini Tank Buns if possible.<br/>Pumplich (100 HP, 50 atk, 30 def) can be turned into a Pumplich Bun for its wallpass ability.

### 33-39F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Megataur</span> (31-40F), <span class="redText">Polygon Singa</span> (31-50F), <span class="redText">Skullmancer</span> (35-37F), Popdillo (36-40F)</p>

The monster table shrinks to 4\~5 slots in this floor range, so expect to encounter lots of the same foes.<br/>You can safely farm items with a Sanctuary Scroll + Peach Bun on 33-34F or 38-39F if desired.<br/>Skullmancer's magic can be extremely dangerous, so consider using a Peach Staff charge against it.<br/>Eating a Skullmancer Bun lets you use powerful magic, and Popdillo Bun lets you farm more items.<br/>It's possible to avoid Polygon Singa's Max Fullness decrease by staying in Morphed status.<br/>Remember that you can throw a Peach Bun of a weaker monster at a foe to turn them into that monster,<br/>and all of the monsters on this floor range can be ensnared by traps.

Polygon Singa (129 HP, 40 atk, 40 def) decreases Max Fullness by 7 when adjacent.<br/>Popdillo (100 HP, 51 atk, 45 def)'s tackle makes you trip and drop inventory items on the ground.<br/>Skullmancer (111 HP, 50 atk, 41 def)'s magic effects include Paralyzed, Sealed, Onigiri, Confused, Slow, or Level-1.<br/>However, the Confused status effect changes to Disguised status when the target is not the player.

### 41-50F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Polygon Singa</span> (31-50F), <span class="redText">Mini Tank</span> (40-41F, 50-51F), Hyper Gazer (40-50F), <span class="redText">Skullmancer</span> (41-43F, 47-49F),<br/>Megahead (41-50F), <span class="redText">Lv4 Ninjas</span> (41-59F), Terror Scorpion (41-60F), <span class="redText">Lv4 Monks (not Okina/Tengu)</span> (41-60F),<br/>Lv3 Monks (45-50F), Electroid (45-52F), <span class="redText">Ultra Gazer</span> (50F), <span class="redText">Okina Monk A</span> (50-99F), <span class="redText">Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F)</p>

<h3 class="heading3Tree">Sacred Tree Mode (51-99F)</h3>

### General Advice

The extension uses a combo of Okina + Tengu monks as a base to send strong, hasted foes into your path,<br/>and since both monks appear between 50-99F, you really need to be ready for combat at any given time.<br/>In addition, Ultra Gazers spawn on floors ending in 0, and Gitan Mamels on 55F, 65F, 75F, and 85F.<br/>Hallways switch to being dark starting on 51F, meaning you can't see incoming enemies by default anymore.<br/>The second and final floor range where you can synthesize items is Mixergons between 60-79F.<br/>Overall, if you built a strong foundation in the 1st half and carefully ration resources, you'll likely be fine.

#### Eradication Scroll Targets

- Field Knave Family (10-12F, 23-30F, 51-61F)
    - Ensures that ground items can be claimed, and inventory items are safe.
- Okina Monk Family (16-18F, 50-99F) or Tengu Monk Family (16-19F, 45-99F)
    - Okina Monk is a higher priority, since it sends powerful foes next to you in hallways.
- Abyss Dragon (87-99F)
    - 40 fire damage from anywhere on the floor adds up very quickly.
- Fire Ninja Family (5-8F, 20-23F, 41-59F), Swordmaster (60-70F), and Cursenior (65-74F)
    - These are troublesome, but considering the rarity of Blank Scrolls, they're not worth eradicating.

### 51-59F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Mini Tank</span> (50-51F), <span class="redText">Lv4 Ninjas</span> (41-59F), Electroid (45-52F), <span class="redText">Lv4 Monks (not Okina/Tengu)</span> (41-60F),<br/>Terror Scorpion (41-60F), <span class="redText">Okina Monk A</span> (50-99F), <span class="redText">Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F), <span class="redText">Nimble Knave</span> (51-61F),<br/>Dragon Pit (51-56F), Soul Reaper (51-60F), Admiral Spearfish (53-68F), <span class="redText">Gitan Mamel</span> (55F)</p>

<p><span class="highlightFog">Darkness Ahead</span> - Visibility in hallways is reduced to a 1 tile radius from this point.<br/>The full suite of Lv4 (A rank) monks and ninjas roam around here, so beware of Okina and Tengu monks.<br/>Nimble Knave appears, so never explore while Far-throwing status is active unless you want to lose items.<br/>Don't be stingy against Terror Scorpions, as reduced Max Strength makes fighting everything more difficult.<br/>Soul Reapers and Dragon Pits are fantastic Swift speed monsters to turn into Peach Buns.<br/>Admiral Spearfish Bun can be used to cripple or one-shot certain foes in the 60-70F range.</p>

Nimble Knave (145 HP, 50 atk, 40 def) has Swift 1 speed, and throws Weeds to parry inventory items.<br/>Dragon Pit (130 HP, 54 atk, 40 def)  has Swift 1 speed and tries to sandwich you in hallways.<br/>Soul Reaper (75 HP, 40 atk, 40 def) has Swift 2 speed + wall-clip, and detects you from anywhere.<br/>Admiral Spearfish (200 HP, 59 atk, 50 def) shoots water when it's standing on a water tile.<br/>Gitan Mamel (20 HP, 100 atk, 999 def) has Swift 2 speed, and only takes 1 damage from normal attacks.

### 60-69F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Okina/Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F), <span class="redText">Nimble Knave</span> (51-61F), Admiral Spearfish (53-68F), <span class="redText">Ultra Gazer</span> (60F),<br/><span class="redText">Mini Tank</span> (60-61F), <span class="redText">Swordmaster</span> (60-70F), Mixergon (60-79F), Detonachin (60-80F), Infernous (60-80F),<br/>Gigataur (60-99F), Lordly Samurai (61-80F), <span class="redText">Cursenior</span> (65-74F), <span class="redText">Porgon</span> (66-70F), <span class="redText">Gitan Mamel</span> (65F)</p>

<p><span class="pinkText">Danger Zone #1</span> - Swordmaster and Cursenior are major threats to inventory items.<br/>If you want to synthesize with Mixergon, use a Peach Staff charge and eat its Peach Bun.<br/>The only type of monks that remain after you proceed past 60F are Okina Monk A and Tengu Monk A,<br/>meaning the chance of hasted foes being sent into your path increases - Eradicate Okina Monk A if possible.<br/>Cursenior curses up to 2 items, prioritizing equipment - Stay in Morphed status to ensure safety.<br/>Porgon also appears between 66-70F, but it's almost pointless to collect Porky's Rocks at this point,<br/>since Behemoths don't appear and foes have high HP, though Porky's Rocks can one-shot Gitan Mamels.</p>

Equipment parried by Swordmaster lands somewhere nearby instead of being sent directly behind you,<br/>and losing either the Peach Bun weapon rune or Trapper's Bracelet here would be devastating.<br/>It's best to stay in Morphed status so that Swordmaster simply doesn't get the chance to ruin your run.<br/>If you're out of Swift monster Peach Buns, Admiral Spearfish Bun is likely your next best option,<br/>since its ability cripples Swordmaster, Lordly Samurai, and Gigataur, and can one-shot Infernous.<br/>Consider stocking up on Infernous Buns to protect yourself against Abyss Dragon's flames later on.

Swordmaster (250 HP, 77 atk, 55 def) knocks away your equipped weapon, shield, or bracelet.<br/>Cursenior (205 HP, 65 atk, 58 def) curses up to 2 items at once, prioritizing equipment first.<br/>Detonachin (260 HP, 55 atk, 37 def) explodes when HP ≤ 52 (Deal 208+ damage to it).<br/>Infernous (285 HP, 69 atk, 55 def) is immune to projectiles that lack piercing properties.<br/>Gigataur (235 HP, 85 atk, 62 def) occasionally lands a critical hit when attacking.<br/>Lordly Samurai (200 HP, 63 atk, 50 def) revives as Ghost Samurai (4 HP) after being defeated.<br/>Porgon (200 HP, 85 atk, 60 def) throws 20 damage Porky's Rocks within a 10 tile radius.

### 70-79F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Okina/Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F), <span class="redText">Swordmaster</span> (60-70F), Mixergon (60-79F), Detonachin (60-80F),<br/>Infernous (60-80F), Gigataur (60-99F), Lordly Samurai (61-80F), <span class="redText">Cursenior</span> (65-74F), <span class="redText">Porgon</span> (66-70F),<br/><span class="redText">Ultra Gazer</span> (70F), <span class="redText">Gitan Mamel</span> (75F), Grampadillo (76-99F)</p>

<p><span class="pinkText">Danger Zone #1 (Continued)</span> - Swordmaster until 70F, and Cursenior until 74F.<br/>Like before, stay in Morphed status to ensure safety against Swordmaster and Cursenior.<br/>75-79F is an okay floor range to synthesize or try and stock up on items for the final stretch.<br/>Grampadillo (175 HP, 71 atk, 56 def) makes you trip - Dropped traps can't be picked up again.<br/>Morphed status doesn't protect you from being tripped by Grampadillo, so beware near water.</p>

### 80-85F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Okina/Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F), Gigataur (60-99F), Grampadillo (76-99F), <span class="redText">Miracle Hen</span> (80-89F),<br/><span class="redText">Demon Scorpion</span> (80-92F), Deathead (81-93F), Polygon Stunna (81-95F), <span class="redText">Ultra Gazer</span> (80F), <span class="redText">Gitan Mamel</span> (85F)</p>

<p><span class="pinkText">Danger Zone #2</span> - Draining types and high-powered monsters abound, so rush stairs.<br/>Demon Scorpion lowers Max Strength by 2, or inflicts Weakened status during Morphed status.<br/>Likewise Polygon Stunna lowers Max Fullness by 10, but Morphed status alters the effect to damage.<br/>Hoppin' Slammer (80-95F) is immune to staves, but has lower stats so it shouldn't be an issue.<br/>Miracle Hen has Swift 2 speed, so use Peach Staves on it if you're out of Swift monster Peach Buns.<br/>Deathead has an attack power stat that rivals Gitan Mamel, so don't just trade hits with it.</p>

Miracle Hen (220 HP, 82 atk, 60 def) has Swift 2 speed and very high attack power.<br/>Demon Scorpion (295 HP, 73 atk, 55 def) occasionally lowers Max Strength by 2 when adjacent.<br/>Deathead (282 HP, 99 atk, 60 def) attacks up to 5 tiles ahead and strikes through hallway corners.<br/>Polygon Stunna (229 HP, 50 atk, 55 def) decreases Max Fullness by 10 when adjacent.

### 86-99F

<p><span class="highlightYellow">Notable:</span> <span class="redText">Okina/Tengu Monk A</span> (50-99F), Gigataur (60-99F), Grampadillo (76-99F), <span class="redText">Miracle Hen</span> (80-89F),<br/><span class="redText">Demon Scorpion</span> (80-92F), Deathead (81-93F), Polygon Stunna (81-95F), Tiger Launcher (86-99F),<br/><span class="redText">Abyss Dragon</span> (87-99F), <span class="redText">King Reaper</span> (88-99F), <span class="redText">Ultra Gazer</span> (90F)</p>

<p><span class="pinkText">The Final Rush</span> - Use Swift monster Peach Buns to rush to the stairs as fast as possible.<br/>Direct combat is difficult unless you use traps or a powerful attacker Peach Bun like Bashagga Bun.<br/>Tiger Launcher throws the player at a creature or onto a trap located within 20 tiles.<br/>King Reaper has Swift 2 speed, passes through walls, and detects the player from anywhere.<br/>Use Peach Staves on King Reapers to restock Swift monster Peach Buns for rushing stairs as needed.<br/>Abyss Dragon spews 40 damage flames from anywhere - Eradicate them if you have a Blank Scroll.<br/>If that's not an option, Heat-ban Incense or Infernous Bun provides protection against fire damage.</p>

The monster table condenses down from 9\~11 monsters to 7 monsters for the last 4 floors:<br/>Abyss Dragon, King Reaper, Tiger Launcher, Gigataur, Grampadillo, Okina Monk A, and Tengu Monk A.<br/>Utilize whatever resources you have left to survive, and the clear is yours.

Tiger Launcher (255 HP, 83 atk, 65 def) throws an adjacent creature up to 20 tiles away.<br/>Abyss Dragon (300 HP, 90 atk, 65 def) spews a 40 damage flame from anywhere on the floor.<br/>King Reaper (225 HP, 65 atk, 50 def) has Swift 2 speed, wall-clip, and detects the player from anywhere.
